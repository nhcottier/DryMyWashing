# Dry My Washing Free Organic Marketing Campaign

## Positioning

Laundry weather forecasts made simple.

Know if your washing will dry before you hang it out.

## 30-Day Free Campaign Plan

### Week 1: Foundation

Day 1: Publish the updated homepage and SEO pages.

Day 2: Submit the GitHub Pages URL to Google Search Console and request indexing for the homepage plus all five SEO pages.

Day 3: Post the first short video: "Can I hang washing out today? I made an app for that."

Day 4: Share the app in one relevant local Facebook group using a helpful, non-salesy post.

Day 5: Post a Reddit maker story in a suitable New Zealand or iOS app community.

Day 6: Email two local journalists or newsletters with the NZ laundry weather angle.

Day 7: Review App Store product page keywords, subtitle, screenshots, and first three lines of the description.

### Week 2: Consistent Short-Form Content

Day 8: Post a cloudy-day drying video.

Day 9: Share the "will washing dry today" SEO page in a practical household tip context.

Day 10: Post a rain rescue alert demo.

Day 11: Comment helpfully on Reddit threads about NZ weather, laundry, damp houses, or line drying.

Day 12: Post a before-and-after video showing forecast confusion versus the app answer.

Day 13: Email three more local media contacts.

Day 14: Check analytics and note which page or post got the most clicks.

### Week 3: Community Proof

Day 15: Ask early users for one-sentence feedback.

Day 16: Post a "what feature should I add next?" Facebook/community prompt.

Day 17: Share a short video about drying score and expected drying time.

Day 18: Post a Reddit update with what you learned from building the app.

Day 19: Share the cloudy-day SEO page during a cloudy forecast day.

Day 20: Make one screenshot carousel for Facebook: best window, rain alert, widget, Pro.

Day 21: Update App Store screenshots or metadata if a recurring message is proving clearer.

### Week 4: Repeat What Works

Day 22: Repost the best-performing video with a new hook.

Day 23: Pitch one morning radio show or local newspaper.

Day 24: Share a real-time weather example: "Auckland today: line or dryer?"

Day 25: Post the "how long does washing take to dry outside" guide.

Day 26: Ask for App Store reviews from people who have tried the app.

Day 27: Make a simple FAQ post answering the top three comments.

Day 28: Publish a final month-one recap post: downloads, lessons, and next features.

Day 29: Refresh page titles/descriptions if Search Console shows promising terms.

Day 30: Plan the next 30 days around the top two channels only.
